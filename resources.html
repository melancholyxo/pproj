Resources

  • Health Powered Kids - Online Safety Lesson:
    Age Group: 9-14 years old
    Duration: 30 minutes
    Overview: This lesson explores what it means to leave a digital footprint and discusses what a digital imprint is. It helps young people learn about their own online presence and activity, and make changes if they decide to. The lesson includes an interactive worksheet and online activities.
    Resource Link: Health Powered Kids - Digital Footprint Lesson

    Surfnetkids - What Is a Digital Footprint?:
    Grade Level: 6-8
    Content: Teaching materials including a main video lesson, downloadable student packets, and Family Tip Sheets.
    Resource Link: Surfnetkids - Digital Footprint Resources

  • Stopcyberbullying.org:
    Description: Created by the people at WiredSafety.org, this site provides information geared toward children ages 7 to 17. It covers various aspects of cyberbullying and offers helpful advice on staying safe online.
    Resource Link: Stopcyberbullying.org

    Cyberbullying - How to UnMake a Bully (Video Series):
    Description: Created by middle and high school students, this video series uses a spy movie theme to demonstrate how to fight cyberbullying. It provides practical strategies and empowers kids to take action against online bullying.
    Resource Link: Watch Part 1 and Part 2

  • BrainPOP Educators - Digital Etiquette Lesson Plan:
    Grade Levels: 3-5, 6-8, 9-12
    Description: This lesson plan accompanies the BrainPOP topic on Digital Etiquette. It supports the standard of recognizing safe, appropriate, positive, and responsible online behavior. Students learn strategies to combat negative online behavior. The lesson includes activating prior knowledge, building knowledge through reading and discussion, applying and assessing understanding, and deepening learning through creative activities.
    Resource Link: BrainPOP Educators - Digital Etiquette Lesson Plan

    Kidslox - Guide to Internet Etiquette:
    Description: Explore this guide to internet etiquette, which covers essential online manners. It provides ten tips for teaching kids about netiquette, ensuring their success in the digital world and beyond. Resource Link Kidslox - Guide to Internet Etiquette

  • MIT Media Lab - Family-Friendly Data Privacy + AI Activities:
    Description: Interactive lessons designed to teach kids about data privacy and AI. These activities empower children to be conscientious consumers of technology.
    Resource Link: Media Lab - Family-Friendly Data Privacy

    Privacy Pursuit! Teaching Kids About Privacy:
    Description: An activity booklet with word searches, crossword puzzles, and other fun activities to teach kids about online privacy.
    Resource Link: Privacy Pursuit!
