Online Etiquette 101: Teaching Kids the Dos and Don’ts of Digital Communication

by Diya Aravinthan / 6 March 2024

In our digital world, where screens connect us and messages fly across cyberspace, understanding online etiquette is essential. Whether kids are chatting with friends, participating in virtual classrooms, or sharing their thoughts on social media, practicing good netiquette ensures respectful and positive interactions. Let’s explore the dos and don’ts of digital communication for both kids and parents:

Photo by Anastasiia Krutota on Unsplash

Dos:

  • Think Before You Type: Encourage kids to pause and consider their words before sending messages. Kindness matters online too!
  • Be Respectful: Treat others as you would in person. Use polite language and avoid offensive comments.
  • Protect Privacy: Remind kids not to share personal information online. Keep passwords secure and be cautious about what they reveal.
  • Use Emojis Wisely: Emojis can convey emotions, but too many can clutter messages. Choose them thoughtfully.
  • Check Your Tone: Text lacks vocal cues, so context matters. Remind kids to clarify if their message might be misinterpreted.

Don'ts:

  • TAvoid All Caps: TYPING IN ALL CAPS CAN SEEM LIKE SHOUTING. Encourage kids to use lowercase letters.
  • Limit Exclamation Marks: Excessive exclamation marks can come across as overly enthusiastic or insincere. One or two are plenty!
  • Skip the Drama: Teach kids not to engage in online arguments. If someone is rude, it’s okay to ignore or block them.
  • Avoid Overuse of Abbreviations: LOL, BRB, and OMG are fun, but too many can confuse readers. Balance is key.
  • Think Twice Before Posting: Remind kids that once something is online, it’s hard to take back. Encourage thoughtful sharing.

Parents’ Role:

  • Model Good Behavior: Demonstrate respectful online communication. Kids learn by observing.
  • Discuss Digital Etiquette: Have open conversations about netiquette. Explain why it matters and how it impacts relationships.
  • Monitor and Guide: Keep an eye on kids’ online interactions. Offer guidance when needed.

Remember, practicing good online manners creates a positive digital environment for everyone!
